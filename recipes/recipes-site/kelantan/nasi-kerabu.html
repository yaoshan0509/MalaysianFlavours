Nasi Kerabu

Nasi Kerabu is a popular traditional dish from Kelantan, Malaysia. This vibrant dish features blue rice colored by butterfly pea flowers, served with a variety of herbs, vegetables, fried fish or chicken, salted eggs, and sambal. It's a feast for both the eyes and the palate, offering a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 10 butterfly pea flowers (bunga telang)
  • 200g dried fish or fried chicken
  • 2 salted eggs, halved
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 cup fresh herbs (mint, basil, daun kesum), finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts, blanched
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons sambal (spicy chili paste)
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, finely sliced
  • 2 shallots, finely sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil and add the butterfly pea flowers. Let it simmer until the water turns a deep blue color.
  3. Remove the flowers and add the blue water to the rice. Cook the rice as usual. Set aside once done.
  4. In a small pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Sauté the sliced lemongrass and shallots until fragrant. Set aside.
  5. Prepare the sambal by mixing the sambal paste with fish sauce, sugar, and salt. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.
  6. To serve, place a portion of the blue rice on a plate. Arrange the fried fish or chicken, salted eggs, julienned cucumber, chopped herbs, blanched bean sprouts, and toasted grated coconut around the rice.
  7. Spoon the sautéed lemongrass and shallots on top of the rice, and add a dollop of sambal on the side.
  8. Enjoy your Nasi Kerabu Kelantan, a colorful and flavorful dish that embodies the rich culinary heritage of Kelantan!
